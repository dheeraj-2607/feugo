F15 — The Feugo Buggy

Interdisciplinary student project • Mechanical • Electronics • Control • App
Status: Concept → Prototype
Team: 15 members

Executive Summary

F15 ("Feugo") is a lightweight buggy built around a 150cc Hero CBZ engine with a rectangular steel-pipe chassis, integrated suspension, Arduino/ESP-based electronics, and a mobile-control interface enabling start/stop, key activation, and an emergency kill switch. The project doubles as an educational platform with workshops, driving events, and demos.

Engine: 150cc Hero CBZ
Team: 15 members (EEE / ECE / CSE)
Estimated Cost: ₹40,000
Timeline: ~1 month (post-class)
Open chassis • Modular track demos • Workshops

Objectives & Scope

Design, build and demonstrate a fully functional buggy with advanced mobile-controlled features, safety systems, and community engagement through events and workshops.

  • Design & Build: CAD, chassis fabrication, engine & suspension integration.
  • Control: Arduino/ESP firmware + mobile app for engine control and emergency shutdown.
  • Testing: Modular track testing on campus with strict safety protocols.
  • Community: Host workshops, driving events, and collaborative sessions for revenue & outreach.

Technical Approach & Methodology

Mechanical, electronics, control systems, prototyping and iterative testing.

Mechanical

Open chassis from rectangular steel pipes, integrated suspension with measurement mounts. (Chassis length template: 2m; front width 60cm; rear width 80cm; wheelbase & tyres: to be finalized.)

Electronics & Control

Arduino/ESP-based control, sensor integration (RPM meter), optional LoRa/GSM modules for remote control, mobile app for key/engine control and emergency kill switch.

Prototyping & Testing
  1. Concept & CAD → Component selection → Fabrication → Electronics integration → Field testing → Refinement
